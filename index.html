My Favorite Recipe - Abacha (African Salad)

Abacha, also known as African Salad, is a traditional Igbo delicacy made from cassava. It is savory, colorful, and often enjoyed as a main meal or side dish, garnished with fried fish or meat.

Recipe Information:

Prep time: 15 minutes

Cook time: 20 minutes

Servings: 4

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients:

  • Dried Abacha (cassava flakes)
  • Palm oil
  • Ground crayfish
  • Sliced onions
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Dried black pepper
  • Stockfish
  • Mackerel Fish
  • Garden eggs
  • Ugba (oil bean)
  • Ponmo (cow skin)

Instructions:

  1. Soak the dried Abacha in cold water for 10 minutes until soft, then drain.
  2. Mix palm oil with potash & warm water to form a smooth base.
  3. Add crayfish, pepper, seasoning cubes, and salt, then stir well.
  4. Fry the fish in hot oil until golden brown and crispy. Remove and set aside.
  5. Add sliced garden eggs, ponmo, onions, and Ugba (oil bean). Stir gently.
  6. Mix in the softened Abacha and stir until evenly coated with the palm oil mixture.
  7. Garnish with chopped spinach leaves, fried fish, and ponmo.

Tips:

  • For the best taste, use freshly fried fish and ensure your palm oil is bright and fresh.
  • You can also add Utazi leaves for a slightly bitter flavor balance.
